The Truth Behind – Netflix .vs. VPNs

Oct 25th, 2021

As most of you would know if you haven’t been living under a rock for the last decade that Netflix doesn’t advertise movies and TV shows from other countries without using a proxy or VPN.

But there a mith behind this, a couple of years ago the only way to watch for example watching Dr Who in the Us you had to use a VPN redirecting your traffic to the UK.

But After doing some Research I found the true reason how Netflix blocks your vpn.

But the interesting thing comes in the protocol because as you can see the server has chosen the fastest connection possible.
But if any of yall know how the OSI model and the difference between TCP and UDP protocol.
You get this, but if you don’t let me explain.


TCP:
TCP (Transmission control Protocol) is a widely used protocol for establishing, maintaining and terminating virtual circuits. It’s reliable if a packet is missing it’s re-transmitted.

UDP:
UDP (User Datagram Protocol) is a protocol used for devices and connections that need to be light and quick such a VOIP (Voice over IP) connection. It’s not reliable if a packet is dropped it won’t be re-transmitted but for VOIP is not very necessary.


At the moment my VPN connection is using UDP and for this research it’s working correctly: Let me Explain…

As I told before UDP is a protocol doesn’t re-transmit a packet if it’s dropped which makes it invisible to Netflix’s server. If this would have been TCP the server would have recognized the re-transmitted packet and block the connection completely.
But you might be asking yourself why I made this blog about VPNs and Netflix because almost every person in the world might know this already.

BUT!


Here’s the thing you should look out for if you’re thinking about purchasing a VPN or creating your own on your local machine or server.
If you have the chance to choose which protocol you should use, choose UDP.
This way Netflix’s servers have a harder time blocking your connection and preventing you from enjoying your Movie or TV show.

One last thing before I wrap up, after a couple of uses with the same server or vpn you should clear the history and cache of your browser. Because your browser collects every DNS request your done and save for the next time you visit the site.
So clear your cache and history and you will be up and running in no time.
