Tails – Portable and Secure Operating system

Aug 6th, 2021

Tails is the most secure operating system you will ever encounter because it isn’t like any other operating system like Windows or Macos and even most Linux distros.


Tails turns your computer into a temporary secure machine with all it’s traffic going through the tor network and browser for secure and anonymous browsing. Tails gets recommended by Edward Snowden a white hat hacker that leaked Fbi and NSA documents because he found multiple vulnerability in the Pentagons network.

If you look at the way post-2013 whistleblowers have been caught, it is clear the absolute most important thing you can do to maintain your anonymity is reduce the number of places in your operational activity where you can make mistakes. Tor and Tails still do precisely that.

Edward Snowden


Benfits with using Tails

  1. Avoid Surveillance, censorship, advertising, and Viruses.

    2. Tails uses the Tor network to protect your privacy online and help you avoid censorship. Enjoy the Internet like it should be.

  2. Your secure computer anywhere

    3. Shut down the computer and start on your Tails USB stick instead of starting on Windows, macOS, or Linux. Tails leaves no trace on the computer when shut down

  3. Digital security toolbox

    4. Tails includes a selection of applications to work on sensitive documents and communicate securely. Everything in Tails is ready-to-use and has safe defaults.

  4. FREE SOFTWARE

    5. You can download Tails for free and independent security researchers can verify our work. Tails is based on Debian GNU/Linux.


My thoughts on tails

I use tails personally when I’m at school or anywhere with a public computer it gives me that extra level of security and privacy when I’m using a computer that isn’t mine. At school it was a bit more difficult because I tried to ask permission but they don’t understand that it boots from an USB instead from the hard disk. I also love that it erases itself when it reboots or shuts-down.


Using Tails

Tails isn’t a great replacement for a distro like Ubuntu but for something quick like transfer a file between a colleague or fellow hackers with their tor file sharing system that is preinstalled. And for accessing the dark web using the tor browser it is even more secure because it looks like your using another machine even though it’s the same USB thumb drive. All around it’s a good distro for staying anonymous on another computer or even your personal PC on a public network

I recommend Tails for all your secure and anonymous needs
