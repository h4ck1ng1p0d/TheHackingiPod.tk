Right To Repair!

Aug 15th, 2021

Today I’m going to talk about the right to repair of your devices and electronics.

Everybody has a right to repair their own devices even the most difficult ones should have a way to repair them even if it takes more time and patience.


What do I mean by that?

Before telling you what I mean let me ask you this:

Would you buy a bike if you couldn’t fix the chain


If you have a bike that you buy with the money you’ve worked for or you received as a gift and your chains breaks and your not allowed to replace it or fix it, and if you do you will get fined of arrested. I may sound pretty overkill right now but you get what I am.

What the manufacture wants is when something breaks on your bike you need to buy a new bike which you might cost a lot. And they try to shutdown shops and repair businesses.


The Quote I love is You Bought it, You own it. It’s logical that when you’ve paid for it means you have ownership you should be able to hack, repair, upgrade without getting into trouble or being fined.

For example with Apple, there was and still is a argument about the 2013-2014 macbook pro with retina display about the red light on the motherboard which apple claims that when those light turn red it means the machine has come in contact with a liquid. But researchers have found that these sensors are so sensitive that they activate in a very humid environment. Which results to a non functioning display backlight.

But when you brought it to apple the genius bar would say this:


Well, this may be a couple of reason: it could be the display or the motherboard because it may be water damaged

And this would result to a 2000 bucks charge and after you bring it to a 3rd party after opening it up they’ve figured out that it was a display connection issue because the display was working but the backlight wasn’t turning on.
