Don't use a VPN!!!

Aug 8th, 2021

This blog is going to talk about Your Online Security, I’m going to tell you:

  1. What internet security exists without a VPN?
  2. Legitimate reasons to use a VPN.
  3. Things that a VPN CANNOT do.


Let’s start with what internet security your already have without using a VPN.

Your ISP can see which websites you visit
for example you go to YouTube to watch a video you open your browser type in the URL and hit enter. What ISP sees is the same thing but they don’t what kind of video your watching but they do know you accessed YouTube.

HTTPS secures most websites connections
Every website in 2021 uses HTTPS which means your connection with that website is already encrypted so if you see the little lock in the top left of your address bar this means that the website and your data is encrypted.

Your online activity is always being tracked
Your activity online is tracked by companies using Cookies, the Facebook pixel, the chrome browser but mostly bu cookies. Companies use this trackers to identify what your interested in so they can use that data for advertising and marketing goals which they might resell to other companies or advertisers.

IP addresses get assigned to every device
This address is being used to identify your device on the network but also your geographical location so streaming services such as Netflix and Hulu can block geo restricted countries such as China and other countries.

Security & privacy is a product of TRUST
Giving your data to companies is a thing you want to control yourself limiting and blocking the amount of companies that can use your data. But this is something of TRUST you can’t be 100% anonymous on the internet. And sometimes you have to give up your information to website with login information and you trust them if they secure their network or secure they servers.


What does a VPN do?

  1. Combat Censorship
    2. Most uses for a VPN is to bypass censorship as I may recall earlier a vpn can be used to bypass state censorship like in china, where every major site such as: Facebook, Instagram, twitter, YouTube, etc. We use everyday are blocked due the government not allowing you to access those sites. Well a vpn can get around that.

  2. Location Spoofing
    3. a vpn is a good tool to spoof your location making it look like your in another country, this can allow you to see georestricted content on like Netflix or Hulu. But also in cases I use it to make I look like I’ve never left my home country. But the thing a VPN really can do and that 1 of the reasons I use a VPN.

  3. It Encrypts Your data
    4. As I recall earlier your internet security is already pretty secure with websites using HTTPS. But using a vpn to access your bank is just going to act as a safety net like a redundant layer of protection so not very useful. But something a vpn does encrypt is your DNS (Domain Name Service) this is a server or service that gives the IP address of the web server your trying to access so if you type facebook.com your DNS will look for that address and reply with the IP address to the web server. The thing that is helpful with your VPN encrypting your DNS requests is that your: ISP, University or school network, any public network can’t see what websites your trying to access.


False information about VPN’s

  1. it isn’t a internet connection
    2. a vpn isn’t an internet connection you still have to be connected to the internet it just creates another layer of security on top of the security that network already have.

  2. It isn’t a cloak of invisibility
    3. all vpn companies claim they don’t keep any logs of your data and that if you were doing something illegal like Hacking someone and stealing their information that It wouldn’t be logged. But companies don’t really show any information about logging their users.

  3. it doesn’t protect you from viruses and malware
    4. As companies tell you a vpn creates a secure encrypted tunnel between you and the internet but this also means viruses and malware. So this doesn’t make you invisible to hackers serving malware to your computer.


The goals of this blog the goals of this blog was to showing you that a vpn doesn’t work as the companies that made them actually works, but the problem with our internet security not being as secure as we want is because YOU are the problem. If you receive a email from a weird email address with a weird link in it you going to think twice before clicking on it. Or you visit a website that doesn’t have HTTPS and you download some software of it you’re gonna think twice before downloading anything.
